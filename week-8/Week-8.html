New sketches for simplified design: ![alternate text: Collector cross section](./Collector_crossSection.JPG) Weed needed to offset the point where the rotation bar attaches to the collector to allow reflected light down through the center hole. We opted to drive the rotation bar with a belt drive system due to increased cost efficiency, simplicity, and assumptions surrounding the collector that its mass would not be substantial enough to make back drive a major concern. ![alternate text: Rotating Base](./Rotating_Base.JPG) ![alternate text: Entire new design](./Entire_Design.JPG) Having to mount the storage box to the actual rotating base provided several orientation problems. The beam of sunlight directed into storage is going to change throughout the day as the dish rotates which reduces transfer if not hitting perpedicular to collection surface. We also still need to be able to cook/extract heat from the top, so out incoming sun could either be directed into the side or the bottom of the box. ## November 10: Focuses: Rotating base Model design and material selection. I specifically helped with updating the spur gear (small coral gear) and outer gear (large pink gear).
